Paris Climate Accord

What is the Paris Climate Accord?

The Paris Climate Accord is an agreement within the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC) dealing with greenhouse gas emission mitigation, adaptation and finance. In the Paris Agreement, each country determines, plans and regularly reports its own contribution it should make in order to mitigate global warming. Previously, all UNFCCC member states have signed the Paris Climate Accord apart from Syria and Nicaragua. On 1 June 2017, U.S. President Donald Trump announced that the United States would withdraw from the agreement.
